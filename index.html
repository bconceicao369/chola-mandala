A Brief Introduction

The Chola Prince

Mr. A. Amithaab is the current Chola prince of Anaimalai, Tamil Nadu, and the founder of Akhila Vishw Bharathiya Seva Samaj. He is also the proprietor of ABB TV, the Chief Operating Officer of Amara, and the Chief Executive Officer of Ahura Tech Solutions.

After the dissolution of the Chola Empire, the royal family settled at Anaimalai and was given the title 'Kondai Katti Thondai Mandala Velaalan' — "the royal family with hair tied in a bun like Lord Shiva, ruling Chennai and the surrounding regions."

At the age of sixteen he formed a strong bond with the divine. He was given a task by forces unseen — chosen for a higher purpose: to save humanity from the nuclear war that may break out at any time. Trained by the divine to perceive and channel cosmic energies, he was soon joined by a group of like-minded youth who continue to serve selflessly under his leadership to this day.

Executive Summary

Our Mission & Vision

✦

To reestablish Bharata — currently known as India — in its full glory: first by protecting our motherland, then by reenergizing our spiritual epicenters such as Hebbya, Hampi (Kishkinda) and Ayodhya, and finally by balancing the human, environmental and spiritual energies of this planet.

🛡️

Protect the Motherland

Shielding Bharata and her people from negative energies, hostile nations and the threat of nuclear war through spirituo-mystic and socio-political processes.

🔥

Reenergize the Sacred

Through Rajasuya Maha Yagnas, Vishwa Shanthi Parama Yagnas, idol consecrations and Shiv lingam installations at the nation's ancient energy centers.

🌍

Balance the Planet

Restoring harmony between human, environmental and spiritual energies — addressing global warming, drying river beds and ecological decline.

🕊️

Establish Peace

Spreading harmony among all people irrespective of religion, race, caste, creed or gender by manifesting positive cosmos through spiritual programs.

Since a divine ordainment in 2005, our yagnas and processes have been credited with helping to enable:

  • Torrential rainfall to drought-stricken areas across the land.
  • Economic recalibration to narrow the rich–poor gap across the planet.
  • Economic pressure to deter military and terror attacks against Bharat from hostile countries and organizations.
  • Political realignment in unfriendly nations, including government shutdowns abroad.
  • Inviting and interacting with benevolent extra-galactic and inter-stellar beings to address the planet's most insolvable problems — global warming, drying river beds, a poisoned earth and over-population.

In light of recent military and terrorist attacks, the Samaj has pledged to offer its mystic and psychic abilities in a professional manner to aid and protect the interests of the nation's military and defence establishments — all achievable with a minuscule share of the national defence budget.

Past to Present

The Journey

✦

A timeline of an unbroken spiritual mission and selfless service — from a single fire ritual in 2005 to a movement spanning the nation and the world.

2005

The Mission Begins

'Akhila Vishw Bharathiya Seva Samaj' is registered at Mysuru by Prince A. Amithaab, who begins conducting the Rajasuya Maha Yagna on every new moon and full moon to protect Mother Nature from negative energies.

2006

Meditation for All

Meditation classes are offered to the inmates of Mysuru Jail to purify the inner conscience, alongside public meditation classes for the city of Mysuru.

2007–08

Beggar-Free City — Mysuru

At just 23, the Prince leads a welfare project sheltering and rehabilitating beggars, putting an end to child trafficking, the begging mafia and poverty-driven crime. The Deputy Commissioner's office declared it a success in March 2008.

2008

Hebbya & Secured Mysuru

Bhoomi puja of the Matha Gandhareshwari temple at Hebbya is performed by Sri Vishwesha Thirtha swami of Pejavara Adokshaja Matha. The 'Secured Mysuru' rally brings CCTV cameras and metal detectors to public places city-wide.

2009

₹1,850 Crore for Mysuru

Through the NURM project, the Prince and his trust pressure authorities to release stagnant central funds — building the majestic new KSRTC bus stand at the heart of the city.

2010–11

Diplomacy for Peace

Diplomatic talks in Sri Lanka with the Chief Justice and Members of Parliament. In 2011, Palestinian Minister Mr. M. H. Zuhair attends the Rajasuya Maha Yagna and a public conference on the threats of nuclear weapons.

2012

Guarding the Borders

The Rajasuya Maha Yagna is conducted at eight places bordering India — from Jammu and Assam to Kanyakumari, Lakshadweep and Dwaraka. The Siddharameshwara temple at Hebbya is renovated at the Prince's own expense.

2013–14

An Energy Shield at the Frontier

Yagnas are held at army camps in Ladakh, Tawang and Kargil to create a protective energy shield for our soldiers. Through 2014, a year-long Mahayagna forms a reservoir of Cosmo energy to prevent major mishaps worldwide.

2015

Jammu & Uzbekistan

A temple of Adi Rakshana Jwala Narasimha is built in Jammu on a site once destroyed by a terror attack. The mission then travels to Tashkent and Samarkand, seeking to install protective idols across the continent.

2016

Shiv Lingams Across Bharata

Around 20 naturally-formed Shiv lingams from the Narmada river bed are installed across Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, Delhi and Uttar Pradesh, including an Atma lingam at the Elephanta Caves.

2017

Angkor Wat & the PMO

An atma lingam is installed at the UNESCO site of Angkor Wat under special permission — a major energy breakthrough. In Delhi, the Prince submits a letter to the Prime Minister's Office seeking support for the mission.

2018–19

Varanasi & Beyond

A Shiv Lingam is installed at the sacred pilgrimage of Varanasi, with more across the Western Ghats. In 2019 the Adi Rakshana Dhana Lakshmi Prathyangira idol is installed in Mysuru, as plans advance for an inter-faith temple at Hebbya.

Cosmic Explanations

The Sacred Science

✦

What the ancients called devathas, our age calls by other names. The fire ritual is not mere ceremony — it is a lost technology for tapping the cosmic energies of creation.

"The yogis in the Himalayas do not sit in caves for mental peace alone — they work in the cosmic plane."

The operations of the Prince and his team are deeply connected to spirituality — to communication with higher-dimensional beings and divine souls. In our religions they are called Devathas, Farishtas and Angels; in the language of science, positive aliens, inter-galactic species and extra-terrestrials.

The human brain is a transmitter and receiver of energies, and the pineal gland is its instrument for astral communication — a faculty the ancients kept active and which modern life has dimmed. The mathematicians and sages of old, like Aryabhatta, mapped the heavens without telescopes because they knew technologies now lost to us.

Spiritual ceremonies — fire rituals, the installation of idols — were never mere religious practice. To the true yogi they are a science: a way to tap and direct the cosmic spiritual energies of the universe for the protection of all life.

A Message to the Reader

The Prince's Pledge

The use of nuclear weapons upon any nation would devastate all life — mutating flora and fauna for centuries, poisoning air, water and soil, and condemning generations yet unborn. Keeping this in mind, Prince A. Amithaab and the members of Akhila Vishw Bharathiya Seva Samaj have striven, since 2005, to prevent the outbreak of nuclear war.

The Prince has placed a proposal before the Government of India: should the Government extend its full support to construct the temple complex at Hebbya, he promises an outstanding positive result toward establishing peace and harmony across the globe — evident within 48 days of the idol consecrations.

"Should I fail to provide the results I have promised, I pledge to donate the entire property at Hebbya — together with the constructed temples and their idols — to the Government, expecting nothing whatsoever in return. I would not wish to hold any authority over the land or the temple."
— Prince A. Amithaab
